Our Mission

Closing the Loop
on Food Waste

PPP is a social enterprise designed to be money-neutral, creating a sustainable cycle that turns suburban food waste into compost, and that compost into community gardens in Pittsburgh's food deserts.

Using a Cradle to Cradle approach, nothing goes to waste. This means that excess soil is sold to local farmers, and surplus produce reaches families through farmers' markets.

The Process

How It Works

A four-step cycle that turns waste into community wealth.

01

Collect

We pick up organic food waste from suburban households and drop-off sites across Allegheny County.

02

Compost

Volunteers process waste at a centralized facility, transforming scraps into nutrient-rich soil.

03

Grow

Soil is used to build and maintain community gardens in Pittsburgh's food deserts.

04

Sustain

Excess soil is sold to farms. Surplus produce goes to farmers' markets. The cycle continues.

Our Impact

Why Pittsburgh
Needs PPP

Food deserts affect real neighborhoods. Our gardens bring fresh food, community connection, and environmental relief.

Homewood

Among the highest food insecurity rates in Pittsburgh. PPP plans to establish its first community gardens here.

Hilltop

Residents face limited access to fresh food. Community gardens can bring sustainable nutrition to the neighborhood.

Glen Hazel & Beyond

As PPP grows, additional food desert neighborhoods will receive gardens, extending the cycle of renewal.
