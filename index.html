Our Mission

Closing the Loop on Food Waste

PPP is a social enterprise designed to be money-neutral, creating a sustainable cycle that turns suburban food waste into compost, and that compost into community gardens in Pittsburgh's food deserts.

Using a Cradle to Cradle approach, nothing goes to waste — excess soil is sold to local farmers, and surplus produce reaches families through farmers' markets.

The Process

How It Works

1. Collect

We pick up organic food waste from suburban households and drop-off sites across Allegheny County.

2. Compost

Volunteers process the waste at a centralized composting facility, transforming scraps into rich soil.

3. Grow

Nutrient-rich soil is used to build and maintain community gardens in Pittsburgh's food deserts.

4. Sustain

Excess soil is sold to farms. Surplus produce goes to farmers' markets. The cycle continues.

Our Impact

Why Pittsburgh Needs PPP

Food deserts affect real neighborhoods. Our gardens bring fresh food, community connection, and environmental relief to the areas that need it most.

Homewood

Among the highest food insecurity rates in Pittsburgh. PPP plans to establish its first community gardens here.

Hilltop

Residents face limited access to fresh food. Community gardens can bring sustainable nutrition directly to the neighborhood.

Glen Hazel & Beyond

As PPP grows, additional food desert neighborhoods will receive gardens, extending the cycle of renewal.
